# 📸 WHERE TO PUT YOUR PHOTOS

Drop your photos into these folders with **these exact filenames**.
Until a photo is added, the website shows a striped placeholder telling you which file is missing — so you can't get it wrong.

Use JPG, landscape orientation, ideally 1600–2400px wide, under ~400KB each (compress at tinypng.com so the site loads fast).

| Folder | Filename | What to put there | Used in |
|---|---|---|---|
| `hero/` | `hero.jpg` | Your single best, most cinematic race shot (wide/landscape). This is the first thing they see. | Hero section, top of page |
| `case-tfn/` | `tfn.jpg` | Best Tour of Nilgiris shot — peloton on the road | Case study 01 |
| `case-asian-mtb/` | `asian-mtb.jpg` | Best Asian MTB Championship shot — rider action | Case study 02 |
| `case-bbch/` | `bbch.jpg` | Best BBCH shot — start line / group of riders | Case study 03 |
| `media-partner/` | `mmm.jpg` | Man Made Machine crew at work — camera on track, filming, drone, edit setup | Media partner section |
| `track-championship/` | `track.jpg` | Indian track cyclists / velodrome shot (team pursuit works great) | Asian Track Championship section |
| `about/` | `founder.jpg` | Parshuram mid-race or with his bike — athletic, credible, not a corporate headshot. Ironman finish-line or TFN race shot is perfect. | Founder section |
| `gallery/` | *(reserved)* | Extra shots if we later add a photo strip | Not used yet |

## Tips
- **Photos with real riders you shot > stock.** The whole pitch says "real race-day storytelling, not stock content" — the photos must prove it.
- Avoid photos with heavy watermarks.
- The hero and track photos are cropped wide (banner-style), so pick shots where the action is horizontally centered.
